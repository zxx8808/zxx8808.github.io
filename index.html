TE-20170527-6

发表于 2018-04-19
  1. impeachment n. 弹劾；检举，控告；怀疑

  2. delusion n. 迷惑，欺骗；错觉；幻想

    The impeachment delusion

  3. topple vi. 倾倒；倒塌；摇摇欲坠 vt. 推翻；颠覆；使倒塌

    Schemes to topple the president quickly would hurt the country

  4. frightening adj. 令人恐惧的；引起突然惊恐的

    America has elected a man of frightening impatience as president.

  5. stern adj. 严厉的；苛刻的

    • Four months into the Trump presidency his sternest critics seem ready to tear the country apart.
    • Mr. Monroe issued a stern warning to those who persist in violence.
      门罗先生向那些顽守暴力之徒发出严厉警告。
    • sterner penalties for drug offences 对毒品犯罪的更为严厉的惩罚

  6. revel [‘rev(ə)l] vi. 狂欢；陶醉; 沉湎

  7. yearn [jɜːn] 美 [jɝn] vi. 渴望，向往

    They revel in leaks that batter the White House almost nightly and yearn for the wheels of justice to spin as fast. 他们沉迷于白宫几乎每晚都要面对的漏洞，渴望正义的轮子快速旋转。

  8. spin [spɪn] vi. 旋转；转动; 晕眩; 纺纱 vt. 使旋转 n. 旋转

    • The latest disks, used for small portable computers, spin 3,600 times a minute. 用于小型手提电脑的最新磁盘每分钟转动3600次。
    • He spun the wheel sharply and made a U turn in the middle of the road. 他猛打方向盘，在路中间掉了个头。

  9. sceptic [‘skeptɪk] n. 怀疑论者

  10. pundit [‘pʌndɪt] n. 权威; 专家

  11. tyrannical [tɪ’rænɪk(ə)l; taɪ-] adj. 残暴的；暴君的；专横的

    On the Trump-sceptic right pundits call the president a tyrannical “child upon the throne”. 特朗普怀疑论权利专家称总统是一个暴虐的“宝座上的孩子”。

  12. discharge [dɪs’tʃɑːdʒ]
    vt. 履行(职责)；批准离开; 释放
    vi. 排放；卸货；流出
    n. 准许离开；出院；退伍；排放

    • MrTrump is “unable to discharge the powers and duties of his office”.特朗普“无法履行其职责的权力和义务”。
    • He has a broken nose but may be discharged today. 他鼻梁断了，但今天可能获准出院。
    • Dunning was conditionally discharged for two years. 邓宁被有条件释放两年。
    • Sewage is discharged directly into the sea. 污水被直接排放到海里

  13. hurdle [‘hɜːd(ə)l] n. 障碍，困难；栏 vt. 克服 vi. 克服；跳过

  • the legal and political hurdles that could delay the impeachment or dismissal of Mr Trump for
    years
  • Finding enough money for the project was the first hurdle. 第一道难关是为该项目筹措足够的资金。
  • He hurdled the fence and ran off down the street. 他跨过围栏沿那条街逃走了。

  1. dismissal [dɪs’mɪsl] n. 解雇；免职

  2. splinter [‘splɪntə] n. 碎片 vi. 分裂；裂成碎片 vt. 使分裂；使裂成碎片

    they risk splintering the same democratic order that they want to save.他们冒着分裂他们想要拯救的民主秩序的风险。

  3. slip [slɪp] vi. 滑动；下降；溜走；失足；vt. 使滑动；使下降；摆脱；偷偷地给 n. 滑，滑倒；纸条；差错；下跌

    • It is not enough to point to opinion polls that show public approval of the president slipping each week. 仅指出民意调查显示总统满意度每周下滑是不够的。
    • He had slipped on an icy pavement. 他在结冰的人行道上滑倒了。
    • Shares slipped to $1.17. 股票下跌至$1.17。
    • In June, producer prices slipped 0.1% from May. 六月份，生产商的价格从五月份下降了0.1%。
    • Robert had slipped her a note in school. 罗伯特上学时偷偷地塞给了她一张字条。
    • We must be well prepared, there must be no slips. 我们必须好好准备，一定不要有差错。

  4. sentiment [‘sentɪm(ə)nt] n. 感情，情绪；观点

    • He’s found growing sentiment for military action. 他已经发现支持军事行动的情绪在日益增长。
    • I must agree with the sentiments expressed by John Prescott. 我必须同意约翰·普雷斯科特表达的观点。

  5. proportion [prə’pɔːʃ(ə)n] n. 比例，份额，部分 vt. 使成比例

    • The proportion of women graduates has increased in recent years. 近年来女大学毕业生的比例有所上升。

  6. implacably [ɪm’plækəbli] adv. 无法和解地，难和解地

    • Though proTrump sentiment is softening, the proportion of the country that is implacably opposed to him still falls some way short of a majority.
